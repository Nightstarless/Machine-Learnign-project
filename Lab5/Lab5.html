1.2 Cross-Product Features¶

After looking at all useful features in our dataset, we need to find features that can be combined into cross-product features for the purpose of memorization techniques in wide networks. Finally, we choosed three pair of cross-product features.

[Gender,MaritalStatus]
[BusinessTravel,OverTime]
[EducationField,JobRole]

Gender and MaritalStatus: Gender is an important factor in labour market, and male and female have natural differences in some aspects. Marital status is also a factor that can not be ignored when considering attrition. Gender and MaritalStatus cross-products is meaningful because we can make an in-depth study of employees with different genders and marital status. Certain combinations may have varying correlations to attrition, and that's what we expect.

BusinessTravel and OverTime: These two features seem that they could be logically grouped together. OverTime can increase employee's work pressure, and BusinessTravel is a direct factor to arrtition. Therefore, the combinations of these two features may have postive influence, certain combination may have high possibility of attrition, such as over time and frequent business travel.

EducationField and JobRole: Education field and job role is kind of logically related when it comes to attrition. Under normal circumstances, if the job role of employee is related with education field, this employee we think has lower possibility of attrition. So EducationField and JobRole cross-products will be helpful for us to make better classification we think.

1.3 Evaluation Metrics¶

Genarally, We use accuracy, precision, recall, f1-score, ROC-AUC to evalate the model in binary classification problems.

Accuracy: It is the most common and basic evaluation metric.

Precision: It is the proportion of "true" samples judged by all systems that is truly true, that is, TP/(TP+FP).

Recall: It is the proportion of true that is judged to be TP/(TP+FN) in all samples that are truly true.

F1-score: The metric designed to comprehensively consider the precision and recall.

ROC-AUC: It is the curve of TP vs FP.

But the objective in different situation is totally diferent, which means in determining the most important evaluation metric, it is important to reiterate the business case with our classification model. For instance, email filtering spam, you want it to be more comprehensive (detect all the junk, but there is a lot of useful information is also judged as junk), or you want it to be as accurate as possible (do not always detect useful email as junk)? Obviously, we think that False Positive's damage is greater than False Negative: the loss of important mail if it is sentenced to junk is far greater than the amount of spam in the inbox. However, for the prediction of the earthquake, we hope that recall is very high, that is, we want to predict each earthquake. At this time we can sacrifice percision.

As we stated before, our binary classification problem aims to predict attrition of valuable employees and then let employer know whether employee is at risk for attrition.

Therefore, we believe that we are most concerned about True-Positive and it is important to reduce the number of False-Negatives. In the context of our classification problem, false-negative will cause the situation that the employer and HR don't realize the intention of some employees' attrition. We hope that Employer and HR can use this model to get information that employees' intention of attrition in advance and then make some adjustment to Prevent these kind of situations happening. It would be better to give some employee a false-positive label, for the worst situation.

Given the analysis above of our business case, we think that the most appropriate evaluation metric for our model would be Recall Score. We decided to use Recall as our scoring metric. because it is important that the model's predictions are true-positive, and it is necessary to penalize the model for giving false-negatives.

In addition to deciding on an evaluation metric that is more appropriate for our business use case, we must also determine the best method by which to divide our data into training and testing splits. Our dataset are approximate 1500 instances.

Once again, we are haunted by the curse of unbalanced classes. As far as possible to ensure that classification of data in the test set and training the same proportion, we need to split the data.

First of all, we split our data into training and testing splits by 80%/20%, and the process of splits are stratified. The testing set will be used for the our model and MLP model to compare the performance.

Then, in the cases of class imbalance and the size limitation of our dataset, we would want to use a stratified 10-fold cross validation to help ensure a proper ratio between the classes are represented within each fold and get better performance of our model. We use this stratified 10-fold cross validation set to evaluate our model.
